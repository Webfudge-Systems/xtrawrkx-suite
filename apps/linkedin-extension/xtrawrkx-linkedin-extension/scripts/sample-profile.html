    Detroit just rewrote the rules of EV charging.

    In November 2023, America's first public wireless charging road went live — right on 14th Street in Detroit's Corktown neighborhood.

    And no, you don't plug in.
    You just… drive.

    Here's how it works 👇

    Electreon, an Israel-based tech company, embedded inductive copper coils beneath a quarter-mile stretch of road near the Michigan Central Innovation District.

    When an EV equipped with a special receiver drives over it — the road charges the vehicle. Wirelessly. While moving.

    Think of it like a wireless phone charger — just scaled up to a road.

    🔋 The bigger vision?

    MDOT and Electreon have agreed to expand this to a full mile of inductive-charging roadway in Corktown.
    Michigan Avenue (US-12) is already being planned for the next phase.
    A Ford E-Transit van is being used to test real-world efficiency in 2024.

    Now, let's talk about the elephant in the room.

    💸 It currently costs ~$2 million per mile to install.
    And it only works for EVs equipped with Electreon receivers — not your average Tesla or Tata EV.

    So is this just an expensive experiment?

    Maybe. For now.

    But here's what the optimists see:
    → No range anxiety on long routes
    → Smaller, cheaper EV batteries
    → Fewer charging stops disrupting commutes
    → A clear path toward zero-emission public transit

    Electreon believes costs will fall significantly as the infrastructure scales — similar to how solar went from a luxury to a commodity over two decades.

    Detroit was the birthplace of the auto industry.
    The first concrete mile of road.
    The first three-way traffic signal.

    The thesis is simple:
    Avatars have become a digital identity for millions of Gen Z and Gen Alpha users.
